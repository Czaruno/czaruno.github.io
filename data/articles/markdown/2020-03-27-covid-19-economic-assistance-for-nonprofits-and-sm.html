We have compiled a list of federal, state and local COVID-19 economic assistance programs for nonprofits and small businesses. While the relief situation remains fluid, particularly while legislation moves through Congress, various agencies and organizations are already accepting applications for relief funds.  

We hope you find this list helpful.Nonprofits-

Small Business Administration - Economic Injury Disaster Loans (EIDL) – can be used to pay fixed debts, payroll, accounts payable and other bills that can’t be paid because of the disaster’s impact:

Eligibility                               

No access to credit

Features

2.75% interest rate for small businesses

Long-term repayment options, up to 30 years

Direct loan through SBA with no intermediaries

Application Process:  https://disasterloan.sba.gov/apply-for-disaster-loan/index.html

2. New York Community Trust - NYC COVID-19 Response and Impact Fund – a group of philanthropic foundations[1]have donated $75 million in grants and no interest loans to New York City nonprofits:

Eligibility

501(c)(3) 

Based in New York City

 Recipient of New York City and or New York State government funding

 Annual operating budget of up to $20 million

Track record of robust programming and services for New York residents

Features

Unrestricted funding to support new and emergency needs for services required to respond to social distancing, isolation and quarantine

Technology to support remote workers, securing staffing to assist

Temporary staff to provide cover for quarantined employees, family caregivers and parents

Equipment and supplies including masks, hand sanitizer, gloves and cleaning supplies

Cleaning services to augment in-house operations

Aid the loss of operational revenue from closures, cancelled programs, events, etc. 

Application Process: https://www.nycommunitytrust.org/covid19/

Application for loan not yet available for several days

 Application for grants requires proof of New York City or State funding such as an award letter or contract, and the current operating budget

3. Brooklyn Community Foundation - Brooklyn COVID-19 Response Fund

Eligibility 

Priority for given to nonprofits that work towards racial equity, are led by members of affected communities, and center the voices of those directly impacted by structural racism in decision-making

 Priority given to nonprofits working in the following areas:

Anti-stigma efforts to address anti-Asian discrimination

Prevention measures and support for vulnerable people (older adults, people with compromised immune systems and homeless people)

Food access and meal delivery 

Support for workers to address lack of access to healthcare and paid sick leave, safety equipment and more

Features

Grant up to $10,000

Application Process: https://www.brooklyncommunityfoundation.org/COVID-19-Grants

4. Adirondack Foundation - COVID-19 Special and Urgent Needs Fund – helps nonprofit community-based organizations to financially support vulnerable populations impacted in Northeastern New York.

Eligibility

Priority given to nonprofits provided access to food, childcare services, support for low-wage works and the elderly, and critical media.

 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations, or groups fiscally sponsored by a 501(c)(3) or other charitable organizations. Unable to fund individuals or businesses directly or 501(c)(4), 501(c)(5) or 501(c)(6).

Features

Grants between $1,000 to $10,000 in most cases and a potential for larger grants in special circumstances

Application Process: https://www.adirondackfoundation.org/granting/special-and-urgent-needs-fund-sun (rapid response to make grant decisions within 24 hours)

 

5. Central New York Community Foundation - COVID-19 Community Support Fund – supports nonprofit organizations working with communities who are disproportionately impacted by economic consequences of the pandemic:

Eligibility

 Nonprofits serving Broome County, Chenango County, Herkimer County, Madison County, Montgomery County, Oneida County, Otsego County, Schoharie County

Limited to 501(c)(3), fiscal sponsors or other charitable organizations able to receive tax-deductible contributions such as schools, faith-based organizations and other public entities

Applicants must demonstrate potential to address a basic need (such as food, housing and childcare), to serve vulnerable populations (especially in underserved neighborhoods) and to commit to documenting services provided and impact. 

Features

Potential for renewed funding based on organization’s ability to provide services and documented impact

Application Process: https://cnycf.org/covid19grants#.Xnzl-y9lCfA

  Small Businesses -

U.S. Small Business Administration - Economic Injury Disaster Loans (EIDL) – can be used to pay fixed debts, payroll, accounts payable and other bills that can’t be paid because of the disaster’s impact:

Eligibility 

No access to credit

Features

3.75% interest rate for small businesses

 Long-term repayment options, up to 30 years

Direct loan through SBA with no intermediaries

Application Process: https://disasterloan.sba.gov/ela/

Complete a loan application, financial statement, request for transcript of tax return, schedule of liabilities and monthly sales figures

 

2. New York City Small Business - NYC Small Business Continuity Fund – small businesses located in New York City that are impacted by the pandemic are eligible to the fund as a source of capital:

Eligibility

 Located within one of the five boroughs of NYC

25% decrease in revenues caused by the pandemic

Fewer than 99 employees

No outstanding tax liens or legal judgments

Features

Interest-free loans up to $75,000

Application Process: https://www.surveymonkey.com/r/ZL5N8XL

 Need evidence of drop in revenue point of sale reports, bank statements, quarterly sales tax fillies or CPA certified profit and loss statements, the business’s most recent tax returns (tax year 2019 if available) and demonstration that the business can repay the loan 

 3. New York City Grant Programs - Employee Retention Program - to help businesses with employee retention:

Eligibility

 5 employees or fewer 

Located within the five boroughs of NYC

Business must be in existence for at least 6 months

Features

 Can assist with up to 40% of payroll costs

Application Process: https://www1.nyc.gov/nycbusiness/article/nyc-employee-retention-grant-program

4. Renaissance Disaster Relief Loan - Emergency Small Business Relief Fund:

Eligibility

 For profit entities operating legally in the following NYC locations: Manhattan, Chinatown, Lower East Side, Manhattan East, 32nd Korea Town, Flushing, Jackson Heights, Elmhurst, Murray Hill, Woodside, College Point, Buyside, Brooklyn Sunset Park, Bensonhurst, Bay Ridge and Sheepshead Bay

50 employees or fewer

Demonstrate a 25% decrease in sales due to COVID

Ineligible businesses include loan packaging, speculation, multi-sales distribution, gambling, investment or lending, non-profit, religious or promoting religious activities, political organizations, adult entertainment and independent professional services (insurance, brokers, doctors, dentists, lawyers, accounts and financial services)

Features

Up to $50,000

3% Fixed Interest Rate

Up to 48 months amortization

Up to 6 months 

Application Process https://renaissance-ny.org/emergency-small-business-relief-loan-fund/

Business owner’s photo ID, certificate of incorporation, commercial lease, recent business tax return and/ or recent quarterly sales tax returns, bank statements for November 2019, December 2019, January 2020 and February 2020.

6. Small Business Bank Relief - Banks offering assistance:-

Bank of America – small business customers can request refunds of overdraft, insufficient funds and monthly maintenance. Clients can request to defer payments and refunds of late fees on small business loans. https://about.bankofamerica.com/promo/assistance/latest-updates-from-bank-of-america-coronavirus

Capital One – all customers including small businesses are eligible for assistance including minimum payment assistance, deferred loan assistance and fee suppression. https://www.capitalone.com/coronavirus/

Citibank – small business customers can take advantage of assistance including waivers on monthly service fees, waived penalties for early CD withdrawal, fee waivers on remote deposit capture and bankers available after hours on weekends. https://online.citi.com/US/JRS/pands/detail.do?ID=covid19

Huntington – small business owners can contact the bank to request 90 day payment deferral with no credit bureau impact and suspension of late fees on loan payments. https://www.huntington.com/coronavirus

Santander - small business clients are offered a number of relief options including extensions and payment deferral accommodations. https://www.santanderbank.com/us/branch-services-update-from-santander

Trust – small business owners can take advantage of payment relief for business credit cards and business loans, and the bank is temporarily waiving ATM surcharge fees until April 15. https://www.truist.com/coronavirus-response/banking-solutions

U.S. Bank – small business owner customers can apply for a credit card with 0% APR on both purchases and balance transfers for 20 billing cycles, and reduced pricing on certain loans and lines of credit. https://www.usbank.com/splash/covid-19.html

Wells Fargo – small business clients can take advantage off fee waivers, payment deferrals and assistance with credit card lending. https://newsroom.wf.com/press-release/corporate-and-financial/wells-fargo-announces-aid-customers-and-communities-impacted
