HELP EMPLOYEES MANAGE THEIR INDIVIDUAL PRIVACY: One simple thing you can do is to update your privacy settings by using a helpful tool created by the National Cyber Security Alliance (NCSA).

HOST A LUNCH AND LEARN: Invite outside speakers to talk to employees about why privacy matters. Engage staff by asking them to consider how privacy and data security applies to the work they do on a daily basis, regardless of department. You could even invite your employees to join NCSA’s Data Privacy Day event virtually via livestream on January 28!

CREATE A #PRIVACYAWARE CULTURE: Encourage employees to sign up as Data Privacy Day Champions! It’s free and NCSA will provide a toolkit of special resources. Share messages about privacy around the office, on internal message boards or through available communication platforms.
