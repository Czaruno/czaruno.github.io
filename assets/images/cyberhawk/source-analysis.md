## Implications & Analysis

### The Cybercab as Productive Capital
At lower utilization rates, returns resemble a modest side business. At higher utilization and optimized pricing, the Cybercab looks like a small autonomous enterprise generating $25,000â€“$40,000+ in annual after-tax income.

### 1. Labor â†’ Capital Shift
- For 100+ years, most earned income by selling labor
- Cybercab: buy a machine once, it works thousands of hours/year, earns while you sleep
- Structural shift from wage income to capital income
- Expands access historically reserved for real estate landlords, franchise owners

### 2. The "Micro-Entrepreneur" Economy
- Millions could become small fleet operators
- One vehicle â†’ two â†’ five â†’ a small business
- New categories: autonomous fleet entrepreneurs, infrastructure investors, mobility asset managers
- Dramatically lowers barrier to business ownership

### 3. Wealth Compounding & Inequality
- People with capital can buy more units, returns compound
- $30K entry point vs $500K rental real estate or $1M franchise
- Could be first scalable "productive asset" for middle-class households

### 4. Redefinition of Car Ownership
- Cars historically: depreciating assets and expense-heavy liabilities
- Autonomy flips the equation: car becomes infrastructure, a cash-flowing asset
- New question: "What is the ROI on this vehicle?" instead of "Can I afford this car?"
